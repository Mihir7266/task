About Girnar Mountain

Girnar Mountain, also known as **Girnar Hills**, is one of the oldest rock formations in India and holds immense spiritual, historical, and cultural significance. Located in **Junagadh**, Gujarat, the mountain range rises dramatically from the plains and offers panoramic views, ancient temples, and a divine aura that attracts pilgrims and trekkers from all over the country.

The mountain is sacred to both **Hindus** and **Jains**, featuring religious landmarks like the Ambaji Temple, the Dattatreya Temple, and a group of beautifully carved **Jain temples** on the hilltop, dating back to the 12th century. To reach the summit, visitors climb over **10,000 stone steps**, making it one of the most challenging yet rewarding pilgrimages in India.

Recently, the introduction of the **Girnar Ropeway** — the longest ropeway in Asia — has made it easier for devotees and tourists to access the temples on the peak, offering a breathtaking aerial view of the surrounding Gir forest and hills.

Girnar is not just a religious destination; it is also known for its rich biodiversity and proximity to the **Gir National Park**, home of the Asiatic lions. Whether you're seeking spirituality, adventure, or natural beauty, Girnar is a truly majestic and unforgettable experience.
